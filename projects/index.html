Who Do You Follow?

Many Twitter users mostly follow people who look like them. This app helps people estimate, and therefore change, the gender and racial breakdown of the accounts they follow.

Topics: Gender, Race, Ethics
Skills: R Shiny, Visualization
Addressing Citation Bias

A discussion of the prevalence and impacts of gender imbalance in reference lists, and the ways in which researchers at every level can help address this issue.

Topics: Neuroscience, Gender, Ethics
Skills: Writing, Visualization
Avoiding Automation

A data-driven look into the options available to workers at risk of job automation. Points to greater consideration of skill and knowledge overlap between jobs.

Topics: Job automation, economics
Skills: Writing, R, Data vis,
Emotion in Stranger Things

A sentiment and network analysis of the emotion trajectories of the show's first two seasons. Provides engaging visuals, finds common patterns of emotion, and considers implications for viewership.

Topics: Stranger Things, Sentiment
Skills: R, Data vis, Network analysis
The Neuroimaging Landscape

An analysis of the journal's keyword trends and networks over the past 10 years. Illustrates changing popularity of various topics in the field, and provides an interactive user interface.

Topics: Neuroimaging, Networks
Skills: Web scraping, Python, R, Data vis
Read, Act - Website

A site providing resources to encourage local and national political engagement. Includes a guide for different types of political action, and a link to a related Chrome extension.

Topics: Political action, News
Skills: HTML, CSS
Read, Act - Extension

A Google Chrome extension designed to increase participation in politics. Tracks the time spent reading political news, and occasionally reminds the user to not just read, but take action.

Topics: Political action, News, Web tools
Skills: JavaScript, HTML, CSS
Ranked Choice Voting Simulation

A statistical simulation of the potential results of the 2016 Republican primaries under ranked choice voting, with a blog post explaining the results and providing easily digestible tables.

Topics: Politics, Elections, Voting theory
Skills: Data science, Simulation, Writing
